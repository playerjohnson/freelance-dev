Why Hire a Freelance .NET Developer in 2026

When you need .NET development work done, you typically have three options: hire full-time, use an agency, or hire a freelancer. Here's why freelance developers are increasingly the smart choice for project-based work.

Cost Comparison

A senior .NET developer in the UK commands £65,000–£85,000 in salary alone. Add employer NI, pension, benefits, equipment, office space, and recruitment fees, and you're looking at £90,000–£120,000 per year. An agency charges £600–£1,000 per day. A freelancer? £400–£600 per day — with no overhead, no notice period, and no commitment beyond the project.

For a 3-month project, the maths is clear. Full-time hire: ~£30,000 (plus 3 months to recruit). Agency: £36,000–£60,000. Freelancer: £24,000–£36,000 — and they start next week.

Speed to Start

Recruiting a full-time senior developer takes 2-4 months in the current market. An agency needs 2-4 weeks to source someone. A good freelancer can start within days. When you have a deadline, speed matters.

Expertise on Demand

Full-time hires need to be generalists — they'll work on whatever comes up. Freelancers specialise. Need someone who's done Azure migrations for government? You can find exactly that. Need Entity Framework performance optimisation? There's a freelancer who does it daily. You get depth of expertise that's hard to find in a generalist hire.

Lower Risk

If a full-time hire doesn't work out, you're looking at performance improvement plans, notice periods, and potentially months before you can try again. With a freelancer, the contract has clear deliverables and timelines. If it's not working, you wrap up and move on. No HR process required.

How to Find the Right One

Look for specific experience relevant to your project, not just years of experience. Ask for examples of similar work. Check their GitHub or portfolio. Do a paid trial (a small initial task) before committing to a full project. And talk to their previous clients — references matter more than CVs.

When Not to Freelance

If you need someone permanently, hire permanently. If you need a team of 5+, an agency might coordinate better. And if your project requires deep institutional knowledge that takes months to acquire, a short-term freelancer might not be the best fit. But for defined projects with clear scope? Freelance is hard to beat.
